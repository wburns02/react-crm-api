1. Scope of Service

MAC Septic Services (“Provider”) agrees to provide the following bundled services to the pumped septic system at the Service Address:

Quarterly On-Site Inspection (4 visits per year):

  • Measurement of sludge and scum levels in all compartments
  • Inspection of pump chamber condition (pump, floats, alarms, switches, seals)
  • Air compressor / aerator inspection and testing (ATU systems only)
  • Effluent quality testing (clarity, chlorine residual where applicable)
  • Inspection of inlet and outlet baffles for damage or blockage
  • Visual inspection of drain field condition (surface drainage, standing water, vegetation)
  • Verification of Watchful device telemetry, pump runtime, cycle counts, and air-line pressure (ATU)
  • Chlorine/disinfection tablet replenishment (up to standard dosage; ATU only)
  • Written inspection report with findings and recommendations
  • Filing of all required maintenance reports with the Permitting Authority

Bundled Pump-Out (1 every 3 years, included):

  • Full septic tank pump-out, up to 1,000 gallons — no additional charge
  • Scheduled during or after the third-year inspection of each cycle
  • Pump-out timing may be moved earlier if Watchful telemetry or quarterly inspection identifies elevated sludge or scum

Bundled Pump Replacement (1 every 5 years, included):

  • Full pump replacement — no additional charge up to the tier coverage cap ($3,500 Standard / $8,000 Grinder)
  • Like-for-like or comparable model; if the installed pump exceeds the tier cap (e.g., $9,200 commercial grinder under Grinder tier), Customer pays only the difference
  • Scheduled at the 60-month milestone, OR earlier if Watchful telemetry indicates pump failure / overcurrent / dry-run / stalled rotor before that mark
  • Includes labor and standard fittings; non-standard plumbing modifications quoted separately

24/7 Watchful Monitoring & Notification:

  • Continuous cellular monitoring of pump cycles, runtime, peak amperage, and air-line pressure (ATU)
  • Air temperature monitoring with freeze-risk and heat-stress alerts
  • Immediate SMS notification to homeowner and on-call technician when the OEM alarm panel fires
  • Predictive service recommendations from MAC Septic team based on telemetry trends
  • Customer dashboard access (web + mobile) showing live system health

Priority Emergency Response:

  • 24-hour SLA on alarm-triggered emergency service calls (vs. 48-72 hours standard)
  • Priority scheduling for non-emergency service requests, typically within 24-48 hours
  • Emergency phone support during business hours (Mon-Fri, 8am-5pm) at no additional charge

Provider will contact Customer at least seven (7) days before each scheduled quarterly visit. Emergency dispatch is initiated automatically upon receipt of a Watchful critical alert.

4. Term, Renewal, & Early Cancellation

A. Initial Term. This Agreement has an initial term of sixty (60) months from the Effective Date. The 60-month term is required to support the bundled pump replacement entitlement.

B. Automatic Renewal. Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement renews automatically for successive twelve (12) month terms ("Renewal Terms") under the same terms and pricing, unless either party provides written notice of cancellation at least sixty (60) days before the end of the current term.

C. Cancellation During Initial Term. Customer may cancel during the Initial Term with thirty (30) days' written notice. Early cancellation fees apply per subsections D and E below. Cancellation notice must be submitted via certified mail, email to contracts@macseptic.com, or through the customer portal.

D. Pump Replacement Clawback (Linear Amortization, by Tier) If Customer cancels the Agreement before the end of the Initial Term AND a bundled pump replacement has already been performed by Provider during the term, Customer agrees to pay an early termination fee equal to the unamortized portion of that pump replacement, calculated by tier:
Standard tier: Clawback = $3,500 × (months_remaining_in_60_month_term / 60)
Grinder tier:  Clawback = $8,000 × (months_remaining_in_60_month_term / 60)
Where months_remaining_in_60_month_term is the number of full months remaining between the cancellation effective date and the end of the Initial Term.
Worked Examples Standard tier: Customer signs Standard Pump Chamber Care on January 1, 2027. Pump fails in month 14 (March 2028) and Provider replaces it under the bundle. Customer cancels in month 22 (October 2028). 60 − 22 = 38 months remain. Clawback owed: $3,500 × (38 / 60) = $2,217.

Grinder tier: Same scenario, but with a grinder system. Clawback owed: $8,000 × (38 / 60) = $5,067.

If the same Standard-tier Customer had cancelled in month 50 instead, only 10 months would remain, and the clawback would be $3,500 × (10 / 60) = $583. The longer Customer remains after the replacement, the lower the clawback.

6. Why Continuous Monitoring Matters for Pumped Systems

Catch Problems in Hours, Not Weeks Pumped septic systems and ATUs fail differently than gravity systems. A failed effluent pump can flood a yard within 24-48 hours. A failed air compressor on an ATU kills the aerobic bacteria within 48 hours, requiring a full system reseed. Watchful monitors pump runtime, cycle counts, current draw, and air-line pressure 24/7 — we know about a developing failure before you smell it. Pump Chamber Care bundles the response time and the parts together so the experience is seamless.
What Out-of-Pocket Looks Like Without Coverage Standard effluent pump replacement (parts + labor): $3,500. Grinder pump replacement: $8,000. Standalone pump-out: $625. Drain field saturation from a stuck pump float: $4,000–$7,000+. Aerobic bacteria reseed after 48hr air-pump failure: $800–$1,500. Pump Chamber Care replaces all of these surprise bills with a flat, predictable line item ($149 Standard / $249 Grinder per month).
