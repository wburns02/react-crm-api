# MAC Septic Voice Clone — Recording Script

For Cartesia Professional Voice Clone (PVC). Target: 10–15 minutes of varied conversational audio that exercises every register the outbound agent will hit on real customer calls.

**Persona note:** the script uses "Will" as the agent name throughout, since it's your voice being cloned. If you'd rather the agent introduce itself as something else (Mike, Tom, etc.), just substitute as you read — the clone captures voice, not the name. We can also lean into "Will, owner of MAC Septic" as the persona — owner-calling-personally tends to convert better than generic-scheduler, and it explains away the depth of domain knowledge.

## Recording setup

- **Mic**: any USB mic or wired headset is fine. Avoid laptop built-in if possible.
- **Room**: quiet, soft surfaces if you have them (carpet, curtains, couch). Don't worry about studio quality — phone calls are 8 kHz μ-law anyway, so what matters is **clean signal + natural delivery**, not pristine audio.
- **Distance**: 6–8 inches from mouth, slightly off-axis (so plosives don't pop).
- **Format**: WAV or high-bitrate MP3, 16 kHz or 24 kHz sample rate. Single channel (mono).
- **One take is fine**. If you flub a line, restart that line — don't worry about editing it out, Cartesia handles small artifacts. **Do NOT read like an audiobook narrator.** Read like you're actually on the phone with someone — slightly faster, slightly more relaxed, with natural inflections.
- **Take a sip of water between sections** to avoid dry-mouth clicks.

## Delivery notes (the most important thing)

Cartesia clones what you give it. If you read flat, it sounds flat. If you read natural, it sounds natural.

For each section, the **register** is in italics — match that energy. Pause briefly between numbered items. Don't try to "perform" — talk like you're actually saying these things to a person.

## Section 7 — Explaining septic concepts *(confident, conversational, like you've explained this a thousand times)*

1. "Most folks pump every three to five years. If you've got a garbage disposal or a big family, closer to three."
2. "What happens is, the solids settle to the bottom of the tank, and over time that sludge layer gets thick enough it starts pushing solids out into your drain field. Once that happens, the field's compromised."
3. "Baffles are these T-shaped pipes inside the tank — inlet baffle slows down water coming in, outlet baffle keeps solids from leaving. When one fails, you get solids hitting the field, and that's how fields get clogged."
4. "Aerobic systems — those are the ones with the air pump that runs all the time. Fancier than gravity, but more upkeep. Air pump's gonna need replacing every five, seven years."
5. "If your drain field is saturated, you'll see soggy spots, smell sewage outside, drains will gurgle. That's a system that's overdue."
6. "Real estate inspection is more thorough than a regular pump — we're checking the baffles, looking at the field, sometimes doing a dye test. Takes two, three hours."
7. "We don't recommend chemical treatments. Most of those don't help, and some actually kill the bacteria the system needs to work."
8. "If the field's failed on a thirty-year-old system, you're probably replacing it. If it's a five-year-old system that's failing, something else is going on — usually overdue pumping or hydraulic overload."
