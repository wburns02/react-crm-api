Call Recording Setup
VoIP Selection, Compliance, Policy & Training for Dannia's Outbound Campaign
Why We're Doing This Dannia's outbound sales calls capture two legally significant moments: (1) the verbal "yes" to contract terms and (2) the auto-bill authorization. Recording those moments is our single best defense against TCPA complaints, credit card chargebacks, and "I never agreed to that" disputes. Texas is a one-party consent state (Tex. Penal Code § 16.02), so recording is legal as long as one party — Dannia — knows. Best practice is still to announce recording at the top of every call. This document selects the tool, configures it, and trains Dannia.
Part 1 — Requirements Recap
  • VoIP softphone on Dannia's computer — no physical desk phone.
  • Local Texas caller ID — prefer 512 (Austin) or 210 (San Antonio).
  • Automatic call recording with configurable opening announcement.
  • Cloud storage ≥ 2 years, searchable by phone number, date, and customer name.
  • Webhook integration to POST recording URL + call metadata to Mac Service Platform (FastAPI backend).
  • Compliance: opt-out handling, DNC enforcement, call reason codes.
  • Power or preview dialer to sustain 50–150 calls/day.
  • Call dispositions: voicemail, not interested, callback, sold, DNC, wrong number.
  • Budget: < $200/user/month, ideally much less.

Part 5 — Recording Announcement Script

Texas is one-party consent, so we are not legally required to announce recording. But announcing:

  • Eliminates any ambiguity in a future dispute or TCPA complaint.
  • Covers us if the prospect later moves to an all-party consent state.
  • Sets the professional tone — "this is a legitimate business" — which actually improves close rates.

The announcement should be short, clear, and offered at the very start before Dannia starts talking. Keep it under 8 seconds so it doesn't burn pickup energy.

Approved Announcement — record this as the WAV file
This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes. Thank you.

6 seconds. Professional female voice. Neutral tone, no urgency. This satisfies one-party consent, sets expectations, and doesn't scare the prospect into hanging up.

If the Prospect Objects to Recording Dannia's response: "Absolutely, I understand. The recording is just for our quality and training — nothing is shared outside MAC Septic. If you'd like, I can make a note to delete this call's recording as soon as we hang up. Would that work for you?" Then set disposition Callback + tag no-recording-requested, and Will deletes the recording from JustCall within 24 hours.
Part 6 — Recording Retention, Access & Deletion Policy
6.1 — Retention
All call recordings are kept for a minimum of two (2) years from the call date. Recordings tied to a signed maintenance contract are kept for the full duration of the contract plus two additional years after cancellation. Recordings flagged with disposition Sold are flagged "hold forever" and cannot be auto-purged.
6.2 — Access Control
  • Agent (Dannia) — can listen to her own recordings, cannot delete, cannot export.
  • Admin (Will Burns) — full access, can delete on written request, can export for legal or dispute purposes.
  • No third parties have access to recordings without a signed written release from the customer OR a valid legal process (subpoena, court order).
6.3 — Customer Dispute or Chargeback
If a customer disputes a charge or contract, the admin pulls the recording within 24 hours, exports to MP3 + WAV, and attaches to the dispute packet along with the signed contract PDF and the auto-bill authorization timestamp. The recording is the primary evidence for authenticating the verbal "yes."
6.4 — Law Enforcement or Legal Process
If MAC Septic receives a subpoena, search warrant, or court order for call recordings, the admin (Will Burns) reviews the document with counsel before releasing any audio. Never hand over recordings based on a verbal request. Always require written legal process. Preserve a copy of the request and the response for MAC Septic's legal files.
6.5 — Customer Deletion Requests
If a customer asks in writing for their call recording to be deleted, the admin deletes it within 30 days and sends written confirmation. Exception: if the recording is material to an active legal dispute or fraud investigation, we may retain it under legal hold and inform the customer of that hold. Texas does not currently have a consumer "right to be forgotten" for business records, but we honor reasonable requests as a matter of customer respect.
6.6 — Sensitive Information Redaction
If a call captured credit card, Social Security, or driver's license numbers (even though our call script is designed to prevent this), the admin flags the recording for PCI redaction. We manually mute or truncate the sensitive portion of the audio before the recording is archived long-term. Keep the original flagged version in a locked admin-only folder for no more than 90 days.
6.7 — Annual Audit
Once per year, the admin reviews the JustCall recording archive, verifies retention settings match policy, purges any recordings older than the retention window (except "hold forever" flagged), and exports a summary report to the MAC Septic compliance folder.
Part 7 — FastAPI Webhook Receiver

JustCall POSTs to our endpoint on every call completion and recording ready event. The receiver must:

  1. Verify the HMAC-SHA256 signature header against JUSTCALL_WEBHOOK_SECRET.
  2. Parse the JSON payload (event type, call SID, from/to, direction, duration, disposition, recording URL).
  3. Insert or update call_log row matched by external_call_id.
  4. Write to activity_log so the CRM activity feed shows the call.
  5. Return HTTP 200 within 3 seconds — JustCall retries on timeout.
# app/api/v2/webhooks/justcall.py from fastapi import APIRouter, Request, HTTPException, Header import hmac, hashlib, os from app.database import async_session_maker from app.models import CallLog, ActivityLog router = APIRouter(prefix="/webhooks") SECRET = os.environ["JUSTCALL_WEBHOOK_SECRET"].encode() def verify_signature(raw: bytes, sig: str) -> bool: expected = hmac.new(SECRET, raw, hashlib.sha256).hexdigest() return hmac.compare_digest(expected, sig or "") @router.post("/justcall") async def justcall_webhook( request: Request, x_justcall_signature: str = Header(None), ): raw = await request.body() if not verify_signature(raw, x_justcall_signature): raise HTTPException(401, "invalid signature") payload = await request.json() event = payload.get("event") data = payload.get("data", {}) async with async_session_maker() as db: # upsert by external_call_id existing = await db.scalars( select(CallLog).where(CallLog.external_call_id == data["call_sid"]) ) row = existing.first() or CallLog(external_call_id=data["call_sid"]) row.from_number = data["from"] row.to_number = data["to"] row.direction = data["direction"] row.duration_seconds = data.get("duration", 0) row.disposition = data.get("disposition") if event == "call_recording_saved": row.recording_url = data["recording_url"] row.recording_provider = "justcall" db.add(row) await db.commit() return {"ok": True}

Part 8 — Dannia Training Checklist
Before the first call. Open JustCall Desktop. Verify green mic indicator. Verify the REC badge is visible in the call controls panel. If REC is gray or missing, stop — contact Will immediately.
Verifying recording is on during a call. Look for the red recording dot next to the call timer. If it's missing, say: "I apologize, can I call you right back in 30 seconds? I'm having a technical issue." Hang up, reconnect, redial, use the second call for the conversation.
What to say if announcement doesn't play. Open the call with the script: "Hi, this is Dannia with MAC Septic. Quick heads-up, this call may be recorded for quality and training purposes." Then continue with the normal opener. This manually satisfies the one-party announcement policy.
Tagging a call. After hang-up, JustCall prompts for disposition — always set one. Choose Sold, Voicemail, Callback, Not Interested, DNC Request, Wrong Number, No Answer, or Busy. Never leave blank.
DNC handling. If the prospect says "take me off your list", "don't call me again", or "remove me" — apologize once, confirm removal, then set disposition DNC Request. This auto-adds their number to the DNC list and blocks all future calls. Do not argue or pitch after a DNC request.
What to do if recording fails mid-call. If the red REC dot disappears mid-call, finish the call naturally — do NOT hang up on the prospect. After hang-up, flag the call in JustCall notes: "Recording dropped at [time]." Then text Will immediately with the call SID so he can check JustCall support for recovery.
If a prospect asks "Are you recording me?" Honest answer: "Yes, this call is being recorded for quality and training purposes. Is that okay with you?" If they say no, offer to continue with recording disabled or schedule a callback. Set disposition Callback + tag no-recording-requested and text Will to delete the recording.
Sensitive information. Never repeat credit card numbers aloud. Direct the prospect to the e-signature link for payment capture. If they insist on giving a card over the phone, pause recording using the REC → Pause button, take the card info, resume recording immediately after. Set call tag pci-pause-used for audit.
End-of-day review. Dannia reviews the call log in JustCall, verifies every call has a disposition set, flags anything weird (dropped calls, missing recordings, complaints) for Will. Takes 5 minutes.
Weekly call review. Will pulls 3 random recordings per week for quality coaching. Focus on: opening energy, objection handling, close technique, recording announcement landing cleanly.
