Aerobic Septic System Do's & Don'ts
Why This Matters Your aerobic septic system uses living bacteria to treat wastewater. Everything that goes down your drains directly affects the health of those bacteria. A well-maintained system protects your family, your property, and your investment ($15,000–$30,000+ to replace).
DO
  • Spread laundry throughout the week — max 2 loads per day (one morning, one evening, 2+ hours apart)
  • Use liquid or powder dishwasher soap only
  • Use Scott's 1000 Sheets toilet paper or a septic-safe single-ply — it dissolves quickly and won't clog your system
  • Use toilet bowl cleaner sparingly — every 3 days is fine
  • Wipe grease and oil from pots and pans with a paper towel before washing
  • Periodically check toilets and water softener for leaks — these can flood the tank
  • Use your garbage disposal sparingly — most food waste should go in the trash
DON'T
  • Have "laundry days" — large back-to-back loads overwhelm the system
  • Use liquid fabric softener, OxiClean, pod-style detergents, laundry crystals, or scented detergents
  • Use TIDE (orange container) — it kills the bacteria your system needs
  • Use Charmin, quilted, pleated, or scented toilet paper
  • Use pod-style dishwasher detergent
  • Use toilet bowl additives (the "drop-in" kind)
  • Run bleach loads more than once every 3 days
NEVER FLUSH OR POUR DOWN THE DRAIN
  • Baby wipes, "flushable" wipes, Tucks pads
  • Feminine hygiene products of any kind
  • Prescription medications or pills
  • Paint, paint thinner, or solvents
  • Excessive hair (people or pets) — damages aerators, pumps, and spray heads
  • Cooking grease, fats, or oils
  • Cat litter (even "flushable" brands)
  • Coffee grounds or eggshells
  • Dental floss, cotton swabs, or cotton balls
  • Anti-mold cleaning products down the sink (strong biocides kill bacteria)
Watch Your Water Usage Your system can only process so much water per day. Fix running toilets immediately. Stagger showers. If you're filling or draining a large bathtub (60+ gallons), place a washcloth over the drain to slow the flow — dumping it all at once stirs up solids in the tank and pushes them through before treatment is complete.
SPECIAL EQUIPMENT NOTES
  • Water Softeners: Must be plumbed directly to the pump tank — never to the aerobic treatment chamber. Salt brine kills bacteria.
  • Epsom Salt Soaks & Bath Bombs: Use sparingly. Frequent use disrupts the bacterial balance.
  • Hot Tubs / Pools: Never drain into your septic system. The volume and chemicals will overwhelm it.
  • Garbage Disposal: Minimize use. Solids from disposals accelerate sludge buildup and lead to earlier pump-outs.
Winter / Hard Freeze Protection Put buckets over the spray heads with a rock on top to insulate them from freezing. Running water to prevent freezing pipes in the house is okay as long as it's kept to a minimum, but be aware it will likely cause an earlier-than-expected pump out due to the extra water volume entering the system.
TYPICAL CAUSES OF SEPTIC ODOR
  1. Too much water in a short period — A single high-usage day can "flush" the system, overwhelming the bacteria. This causes a temporary low-grade odor and may trigger an earlier pump-out. Spread water use day-to-day, not all at once.
  2. Chemical overload — Excess soap, detergent, or bleach kills the bacteria. The system will recover in a few days, but the odor can be strong. Limit bleach loads to 1 every 3 days.
  3. Aerator failure — If the aerator (blower motor) stops working, the bacteria can't get oxygen and will die. The odor will not go away until the aerator is repaired. If your alarm is going off or you smell persistent rotten-egg odor, call us immediately.
